# Papers — Reading Shortlist

Ordered list of papers to read. Tick off as you go.

## Starter pack — weeks 1 to 5

Foundations + methodology + 2 EPFL-adjacent classics + 1 hot topic.

- [ ] **Week 1** — Keshav, *How to Read a Paper* (2007) — methodology, 3 pages
  - [PDF](https://web.stanford.edu/class/ee384m/Handouts/HowtoReadPaper.pdf)
  - **Goal:** internalize the 3-pass method before reading the rest

- [ ] **Week 2** — Cadena et al., *Past, Present, and Future of SLAM* (IEEE T-RO, 2016)
  - [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/1606.05830)
  - **Why:** SLAM survey — mandatory baseline for robotics. Pass 1 + Pass 2.

- [ ] **Week 3** — Ijspeert, *Central Pattern Generators for Locomotion Control in Animals and Robots: A Review* (Neural Networks, 2008)
  - [Link](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0893608008000804)
  - **Why:** Classic from EPFL BioRob (Ijspeert), very readable, gives you a first feel for the lab's style.

- [ ] **Week 4** — Chi et al., *Diffusion Policy: Visuomotor Policy Learning via Action Diffusion* (RSS, 2023)
  - [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2303.04137) · [Website](https://diffusion-policy.cs.columbia.edu/)
  - **Why:** Hot topic in manipulation — shows where the field is moving (diffusion models for control).

- [ ] **Week 5** — A recent LSRO (Bleuler) or TNE (Micera) paper
  - **Action:** check the lab websites to pick the most recent accessible paper aligned with medical robotics interest.
  - **Why:** Start tracking EPFL output in your target subfield.
