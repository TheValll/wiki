# 1.7 — Determinant of a Product: Stretches Multiply

Pure-intuition companion to §1.7 of [`../01-linear-algebra.md`](../01-linear-algebra.md). No formulas, no exercises.

---

If machine B multiplies every area by `det B`, and machine A (applied afterwards) multiplies every area by `det A`, then the **combined** machine `AB` multiplies areas by `det A × det B`. That is the identity `det(AB) = det(A) · det(B)` — two stretches in series compound by **multiplication**, not addition.

**The physical image.** Same rubber sheet as §1.4. The sheet enters machine B: its area is scaled by the factor `det B` (with a flip if `det B < 0`). The already-scaled sheet then enters machine A: its area is scaled again, this time by `det A`. The final area is therefore `det A · det B · (original area)`. The super-machine `AB`, by definition, produces that same final state in one pass — so **its** single scaling factor has to be exactly `det A · det B`. Nothing more to prove.

---

### Sign handling is automatic

The sign (orientation flip, §1.6) is bookkept by the same multiplication, for free:

| Machine B | Machine A | Product sign | Physical meaning |
|---|---|---|---|
| `det B > 0` | `det A > 0` | **+** | no flips, no net flip |
| `det B < 0` | `det A > 0` | **−** | one flip, net flip |
| `det B > 0` | `det A < 0` | **−** | one flip, net flip |
| `det B < 0` | `det A < 0` | **+** | two flips, they cancel |

Two flips cancel, one flip stays, zero flips stays unflipped — that is exactly what signed multiplication of real numbers does. The algebra is the arithmetic shadow of the geometry.

---

### Why this matters beyond bookkeeping

This property (`det` is **multiplicative**) is one of the deep reasons matrix multiplication is the *right* way to compose linear machines. The geometric scaling factor of a composition is the product of the factors — a cleanly multiplicative bridge from the world of matrices (rich, non-commutative) to the world of real numbers (simple, multiplicative). This is also why `det` behaves so well under many manipulations: it turns composition into ordinary multiplication, and that is a rare and powerful gift.

---

**What to remember.**

`det(AB) = det(A) · det(B)` is **"two stretches in series compound by multiplication"** — nothing more. The sign bookkeeping (how many flips) is handled automatically by the sign rules of multiplication. This is also the cleanest way to see why non-invertible matrices stay non-invertible under composition: if one of them has `det = 0`, the product has `det = 0`, and no clever pairing can rescue a destroyed dimension.
