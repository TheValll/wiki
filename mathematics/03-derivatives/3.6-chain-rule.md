# 3.6 вҖ” Chain Rule

**What it does:**
Derivative of a **composition** `f(g(x))` вҖ” one function feeds its output into another. The total rate is the **product** of the outer rate (evaluated at the inner function) and the inner rate.

Imagine two **meshed gears**. Gear A is driven by a motor and turns at 3 rotations/second. Gear B is chained to A and turns 2 rotations for every 1 rotation of A. Then gear B turns at `3 Г— 2 = 6` rotations/second. The chain rule says: when functions are chained, their rates **multiply**.

**Formula:**
```
Lagrange:  (f вҲҳ g)'(x) = f'(g(x)) В· g'(x)
Leibniz :       df/dx  = df/du В· du/dx
```
Fast mnemonic for `y = (stuff)вҒҝ`, `y = sin(stuff)`, `y = e^stuff`, вҖҰ:
> "Derivative of the outer, **keep** the inside, **times** derivative of the inside."

**Simple example:**
`f(x) = (2x + 3)вҒҙ`
```
Outer: y = uвҒҙ,   dy/du = 4uВі
Inner: u = 2x + 3,   du/dx = 2

f'(x) = 4uВі В· 2 = 8 В· (2x + 3)Ві
```

**Complex example (triple nesting):**
`f(x) = sin(ln(xВІ + 1))`

Three layers: `u = xВІ + 1`,  `v = ln(u)`,  `y = sin(v)`.
```
dy/dv = cos(v)
dv/du = 1/u
du/dx = 2x

df/dx = dy/dv В· dv/du В· du/dx
      = cos(v) В· (1/u) В· 2x
      = cos(ln(xВІ + 1)) В· [2x / (xВІ + 1)]
```

## Intuition вҖ” The Chain Rule: Meshed Gears Multiply Rates


The chain rule is what happens when one function feeds its output into another. The two functions form a pipeline, and their rates of change **multiply**.

**The physical image.** Two **meshed gears**. Gear A is driven by a motor and spins at 3 turns per second. Gear B is chained to A, and for every full turn of A, gear B makes 2 turns. Then gear B spins at `3 Г— 2 = 6` turns per second.

```
                    в”Ңв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”җ
                    в”Ӯ                               в”Ӯ
   motor            в”Ӯ        gear A       gear B    в”Ӯ        final output
   в”Җв”Җв”Җ>  3 turns/s в”Җв”јв”Җв”Җ>  вӯ’ в”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җ вӯ’ в”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җ>      в”Ӯв”Җв”Җ>  6 turns/s
                    в”Ӯ     rate          rate        в”Ӯ
                    в”Ӯ     du/dx = 3     df/du = 2   в”Ӯ
                    в””в”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”ҳ
                              вҶ‘ chained gears

                     total rate = df/du В· du/dx = 2 В· 3 = 6
```

When two rates are chained, you **multiply** them to get the total rate. That's all the chain rule says.

---

### The three-layer pipeline

A composition `f(g(h(x)))` is a pipeline:

```
        x  в”Җ[ h ]в”Җ>  u  в”Җ[ g ]в”Җ>  v  в”Җ[ f ]в”Җ>  f(x)

      rate of u vs x:   du/dx     (gear h spins this fast per unit x)
      rate of v vs u:   dv/du     (gear g spins this fast per unit u)
      rate of f vs v:   df/dv     (gear f spins this fast per unit v)

      total rate df/dx = df/dv В· dv/du В· du/dx
```

Every stage contributes one factor. The chain rule generalizes to any length of pipeline вҖ” just multiply the rates of each stage.

---

### Why Leibniz notation makes it obvious

In Leibniz form:

```
       df     df     du
      в”Җв”Җв”Җ  =  в”Җв”Җ  В·  в”Җв”Җ
       dx     du     dx
```

The `du`'s look like they "cancel" visually. They don't, technically вҖ” those aren't real fractions вҖ” but the *pattern* is exact, and it's a free mnemonic. See [`3.1-lagrange-vs-leibniz.md`](./3.1-lagrange-vs-leibniz.md).

### The "keep the inside" trick

For quick use on simple compositions like `f(x) = (2x + 3)вҒө` or `sin(xВІ + 1)`:

> "Derivative of the **outer** function, keep the **inside** unchanged, times the derivative of the **inside**."

That is just the chain rule with two layers вҖ” outer rate, times inner rate.

```
       (2x + 3)вҒө           outer: power 5        inner: 2x + 3
                           outer rate: 5В·(В·)вҒҙ    inner rate: 2
                           вҶ’ derivative = 5В·(2x + 3)вҒҙ В· 2
                                          в””в”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”ҳ  в””в”Җв”ҳ
                                          keep inside Г— inner rate
```

---

### The physical reason the rates multiply

If a small bump in `x` causes `u` to move by a factor `du/dx`, and then that bump in `u` causes `f` to move by a factor `df/du`, the bump in `x` has been amplified **twice**. Once at each stage. The total amplification is the product of the two amplifications. Nothing deeper is going on вҖ” it's what happens any time a small signal passes through a chain of linear amplifiers.

---

**What to remember.**

The chain rule is the **gear-ratio rule** for pipelines of functions. When two (or more) functions are chained, their rates **multiply**. The outer function's rate is evaluated at the current value of the inner function, then scaled by the inner function's rate. Every stage of the pipeline contributes one factor.
