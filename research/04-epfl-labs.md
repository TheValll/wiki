# Part 4 — EPFL labs (PhD prep)

Preliminary mapping of the EPFL labs relevant to the [PhD trajectory targeting Sept 2028](../me.md). Two strong areas at EPFL match the long-term interest: **spatial robotics** and **medical robotics**, with a supporting cluster in **mobile / bio robotics**.

This page is a **starting point**, not a final list. Labs evolve, PIs retire, new groups form — re-check before any application round. See [`../TODO.md`](../TODO.md) §4 for the open work to deepen this page.

---

## 4.1 — Spatial robotics

| Lab | Focus | Why it matters |
|-----|-------|----------------|
| **eSpace** — EPFL Space Center | Space systems, orbital debris, satellite missions | Direct ties to ClearSpace; the EPFL hub for space robotics |
| Mechatronics / systems labs at the interface | Hardware-software integration for space-grade systems | Several smaller groups feed eSpace projects |

Space robotics at EPFL is concentrated around eSpace + a few application-driven mechatronics groups. The pipeline is smaller than in medical, so the path is more about **finding the right project** than choosing among many labs.

---

## 4.2 — Medical robotics

Historically a strong domain at EPFL — the depth here is real.

| Lab | PI | Focus | Why it matters |
|-----|----|-------|----------------|
| **LSRO** — Laboratory of Robotic Systems | Hannes Bleuler | Surgical / medical robotics | World reference in medical robotics |
| **Translational Neural Engineering Lab** | Silvestro Micera | Neural interfaces, prosthetics | Bridge between neuroscience and robotic actuation |
| **BioRob** — Biorobotics Laboratory | Auke Ijspeert | Biorobotics, locomotion, exoskeletons | Bio-inspired control, applicable to medical and humanoid contexts |

Medical robotics at EPFL has the depth that spatial does not — multiple labs at the world-class tier, real PhD pipeline.

## 4.3 — Mobile / aerial robotics (supporting cluster)

| Lab | Focus | Why it matters |
|-----|-------|----------------|
| **MOBOTS / LIS** — Laboratory of Intelligent Systems | Mobile robotics, drones, swarms | Strong mobile / aerial tradition; useful breadth even if final target is medical or spatial |

Mobile robotics is not the primary target, but it is part of the EPFL robotics ecosystem — collaborations and seminars cross between LSRO / BioRob / LIS regularly.

## 4.4 — How to read a lab page

For each lab on the shortlist, run the same procedure:

```
1. Lab homepage          ─ what does the lab claim to work on?
2. Recent publications   ─ what are they actually publishing in the last 2-3 years?
3. PhD students          ─ who is currently there, what are they working on?
4. Open positions        ─ are PhD positions advertised? On what topics?
5. Cross-check           ─ does the actual work match the lab's tagline?
```

The gap between (1) and (2) is informative — sometimes a lab's homepage describes the PI's career, while recent publications describe what current PhDs are pushing. **Read the recent publications.**
