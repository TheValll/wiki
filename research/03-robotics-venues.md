# Part 3 — Robotics venues to monitor

The conferences, journals, and preprint servers where robotics research is published. Use this page as the **venue layer** of the [literature survey procedure](01-reading-papers.md) — once you have identified the active people in a sub-area, this list tells you where to look for their recent work.

---

## 3.1 — Conferences

| Tier | Name | Role | Volume / selectivity |
|------|------|------|---------------------|
| Top | **ICRA** — IEEE International Conference on Robotics and Automation | The flagship robotics conference | ~2000 papers/year, broad |
| Top | **IROS** — Intelligent Robots and Systems | Second major robotics conference | High volume, broad |
| Quality | **RSS** — Robotics: Science and Systems | More selective, very high quality | Low volume, deep |
| Robot + ML | **CoRL** — Conference on Robot Learning | Intersection of robotics and deep learning | Growing fast, ML-heavy |

**Reading strategy:**

| Goal | Use |
|------|-----|
| Breadth — broad pulse of the field | Pass-1 sweep through ICRA + IROS proceedings (annual) |
| Depth — high-quality work in your area | Read RSS / CoRL tables of contents end-to-end if your topic fits |

ICRA + IROS = breadth-first feed (most papers in your area appear in one or the other). RSS + CoRL = depth-first feed.

---

## 3.2 — Journals

| Name | Role | Format |
|------|------|--------|
| **T-RO** — IEEE Transactions on Robotics | Flagship journal | Long-form, archival |
| **RA-L** — Robotics and Automation Letters | Short format, very widely read | ~6 pages, often co-submitted with ICRA / IROS |
| **IJRR** — International Journal of Robotics Research | Historic journal | Long-form, slow but prestigious |

Journals lag conferences in robotics — the field publishes first to ICRA / IROS, then sometimes extends to T-RO / IJRR. For pure currentness, watch conferences and arXiv first.

---

## 3.3 — Preprint server

| Name | Role |
|------|------|
| **arXiv `cs.RO`** | Pre-publication channel — most ICRA / IROS / RSS / CoRL submissions land here as preprints, often before the conference itself |

This is where you catch papers **before** the formal venue. Set up an arXiv subscription to `cs.RO` and skim weekly with [pass 1](01-reading-papers.md).

---

## 3.4 — How to use this list

Combine with the [three-step literature survey](01-reading-papers.md):

```
Identify key authors  →  Visit their websites  →  See recent venues
                                                         │
                                                         ▼
                                  This page tells you what each venue is
                                  and where to find its proceedings online
```

For each venue, the proceedings or table of contents is online (IEEE Xplore for ICRA / IROS / RA-L, JMLR-style for RSS, openreview for CoRL). A **pass-1 scan of a recent proceedings** is one of the highest-yield reading sessions you can run — a few hours converts a black-box field into a triaged shortlist.

---

## 3.5 — Summary

| Use case | Where to look |
|----------|---------------|
| Get the broad pulse of the field | ICRA + IROS proceedings (annual) |
| Find selective, high-quality work | RSS proceedings |
| Track the robotics × deep learning frontier | CoRL proceedings |
| Read deeper / archival work | T-RO, IJRR |
| Catch papers before publication | arXiv `cs.RO` (weekly) |
